# Deterministic Chess Decision Benchmark

This project evaluates language models as constrained decision systems rather than free-form chess generators.

## Protocol lock

- Canonical paper configs:
  - `paper/configs/debug/smoke_10_games.json`
  - `paper/configs/pilot/pilot_300_games.json`
  - `paper/configs/main/main_1200_games.json`
- Provider/model identifiers are recorded exactly as submitted.
- Temperature is fixed at `0`.
- Paper-safe mode is `constrained_index`.
- Legal moves are generated by `chess.js`.
- The model selects an integer index only.
- One retry is allowed on invalid output.
- Anti-oscillation control is enabled:
  - recent move window: `6` plies
  - immediate-reversal avoidance: enabled
  - repeated-state risk tagging: enabled
  - no-progress threshold: `12` plies
- Live CPL during gameplay: disabled in locked paper configs
- Post-run CPL enrichment before final artifacts: enabled in locked paper configs
- Provider failures are handled separately from model-output failures:
  - rate limits trigger key cooldown, backoff, and retry
  - fallback is not used immediately on provider failure
- Final fallback is deterministic, non-reversing when alternatives exist, avoids prior-state recreation when safer moves exist, and is explicitly logged.
- Randomness is controlled by a recorded seed.

## Locked paper prompt

Paper mode uses a short UCI-only selection prompt:

```text
Return exactly one integer index for the best move.
Do not output words or punctuation.

Avoid:
- reversing the previous move
- recreating previous positions
- moving the same piece back and forth when reasonable alternatives exist

Legal moves:
0: e2e4
1: d2d4
2: g1f3 [repeat-risk]
```

Recent moves are included when available. SAN is not included in paper mode.

## Research claim

The benchmark is designed to separate action hallucination from downstream decision failure. Illegal actions are never executed; they are rejected, retried once, and then replaced by a deterministic fallback.

The system does not ban repetition outright. It rejects oscillatory model choices only when they reverse the previous move or recreate a prior position while safer legal alternatives exist. Genuine repetition remains observable and analyzable.

## Resume protocol

The pipeline checkpoints move-level datapoints and completed games while the run is in progress. If the process restarts, the interrupted matchup resumes from the next unfinished game. This is intentionally a game-boundary resume policy: completed games are preserved, while an in-flight game may be replayed.
